  </Step>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Risk Management and Legal Protections">

Understanding the law is just as important as working a job when considering **Emergency Response Liberty County how to make money as a civilian**. The Liberty County Sheriff's Office and River City Police Department are highly active in 2026, utilizing improved radar and pursuit technology.

To protect your hard-earned cash, you must understand the "Wanted System" and how it impacts your wallet. Fines for speeding or reckless driving can range from $150 to $1,000, which can represent 10-20 minutes of work.

| Violation | Typical Fine | Potential Jail Time | Impact on Earnings |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Speeding** | $250 - $500 | None | Low |
| **Running Red Light** | $150 | None | Low |
| **Reckless Driving** | $750 | 2 - 5 Minutes | Medium |
| **Evading Police** | $1,500+ | 10+ Minutes | High |

<Callout type="info" title="Pro Tip: Legal Defense">
If stopped by an officer, **roleplay respectfully**. Many officers in ERLC are more likely to issue a warning rather than a fine if you cooperate and provide a valid "in-character" reason for your haste, such as "heading to a shift at the hospital."
</Callout>

Beyond fines, vehicle maintenance is a hidden cost. Frequent crashes require repairs at the **Mod Shop** or DOT assistance. Driving safely not only keeps the police away but also preserves your vehicle's health, saving you thousands in repair bills over a long session.
