<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Response Workflow">

To maximize your efficiency and XP gain, follow this standardized response protocol developed for the 2026 update.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="MDT Intake and Routing">
    When an alarm sounds, open your MDT. Identify the location and the nature of the emergency. Select the apparatus that matches the call (e.g., Ambulance for a "Medical Emergency," Fire Engine for a "Structure Fire").
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Safe Arrival and Positioning">
    Drive to the scene using lights and sirens. Park your vehicle in a way that protects the scene from oncoming traffic but leaves room for other arriving units, such as Police or DOT.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Suppression and Extrication">
    If fire is present, deploy your hose or extinguisher immediately. If victims are trapped in a vehicle, use the spreaders and cutters before attempting medical treatment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Medical Triage and Transport">
    Assess all patients. Use the medical kit to stabilize them. If the call requires transport, load the patient onto the stretcher and move them into the Ambulance for delivery to the hospital.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Scene Clearing">
    Once all objectives are met, return your tools to the apparatus and mark the call as "Cleared" in the MDT. This notifies dispatch that you are available for the next incident.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Safety First">
Do not abandon a call halfway through. In the 2026 system, leaving a scene before the primary objectives are met will result in a penalty to your department XP and may prevent you from receiving the final cash reward.
</Callout>
