<Section icon={Target} title="How to Progress: DOT Rank System">

Progressing through the DOT ranks is the core objective of the **Emergency Response Liberty County dot update**. Unlike the civilian team, where progress is purely financial, the DOT requires the accumulation of Department XP. This XP is earned by responding to calls via the Mobile Data Terminal (MDT) and successfully completing objectives. The 2026 update refined the XP distribution to ensure that more complex tasks, such as clearing major accidents, provide higher rewards than simple signal repairs.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Join the Team">
    Select the Department of Transportation from the team selection menu and equip the Trainee uniform at the DOT facility.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Equip Essential Tools">
    Visit the equipment locker to grab traffic cones, road signs, and the repair kit. These are required for most calls.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Monitor the MDT">
    Open your MDT and look for active calls. Prioritize "Traffic Light Outage" or "Vehicle Recovery" for steady XP gains.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure the Scene">
    Upon arrival, place cones and signs to protect yourself from traffic. The 2026 update improved driver AI response to DOT warning lights.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Complete the Objective">
    Interact with the damaged object or vehicle. Once the task is finished, clear the call in the MDT to receive your XP and cash.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Wrench} title="Advanced Mechanics and Scene Management">

The **Emergency Response Liberty County dot update** introduced sophisticated scene management mechanics. DOT players are no longer just "repair bots"; they are essential for traffic flow. When a major accident occurs, DOT units work alongside Police and Fire & Rescue to manage the environment. The 2026 overhaul added "Scene Protection" bonuses, where DOT players earn passive XP for maintaining a safe perimeter around active emergency scenes.

**Common DOT Call Types:**
- **Infrastructure Repair**: Fixing streetlights, traffic signals, and fire hydrants.
- **Vehicle Recovery**: Towing abandoned or wrecked vehicles to the impound lot.
- **Roadway Hazards**: Removing debris or fallen trees from the highway.
- **Major Accident Support**: Coordinating with SWAT or Fire units to clear large-scale scenes.
