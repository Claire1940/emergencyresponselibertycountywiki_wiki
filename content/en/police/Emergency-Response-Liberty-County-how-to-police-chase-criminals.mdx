<Section icon={Target} title="Tactical Maneuvers and Disable Methods">

To end a pursuit quickly, officers must transition from passive following to active disabling maneuvers. The longer a chase lasts, the higher the risk of the suspect escaping or causing a severe accident.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="PIT Maneuver">
    - **Target the rear quarter panel** of the suspect's vehicle.
    - Apply a gentle bump to force a spin-out.
    - Best executed at moderate speeds on wide roads.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spike Strips">
    - **Deploy ahead of the suspect** along their predicted route.
    - Instantly pops tires to reduce top speed and traction.
    - Requires precise timing and radio coordination.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tactical Boxing">
    - **Surround the suspect vehicle** with multiple police units.
    - Gradually slow down to force the suspect to a complete stop.
    - Requires at least three cooperative patrol units.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Pursuit Execution">

Executing a flawless pursuit requires following a structured tactical protocol. Use this systematic approach to maximize your arrest efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquire Visual and Call for Backup">
    Identify the suspect vehicle, read their license plate, and immediately call out their location, heading, and vehicle model to active units.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Match Speed and Maintain Safe Distance">
    Close the gap without tailgating. Keep enough distance to react to sudden braking, sharp turns, or intentional collisions by the suspect.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute Disable Maneuver">
    Wait for a straight, clear stretch of road or a highway segment to execute a PIT maneuver or deploy spike strips with backup assistance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure the Suspect on Foot">
    Once the vehicle is disabled, exit your cruiser immediately with your taser or handcuffs equipped. Prevent the suspect from fleeing on foot.
  </Step>
</Steps>
