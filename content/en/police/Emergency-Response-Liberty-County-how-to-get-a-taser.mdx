<Section icon={Swords} title="Taser Mechanics and Technical Specs">

The Taser in Emergency Response: Liberty County is designed for realism. It is not a "magic" stun gun that works from across the map. Understanding its limitations is just as important as knowing how to get it. The device fires two probes connected by thin wires. If both probes hit the target, an electrical current is delivered.

| Statistic | Value | Tactical Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Effective Range** | Mid-Range (approx. 15-20 feet) | Probes will miss if the target is too far |
| **Effect Duration** | 3-5 Seconds | Suspect will "ragdoll" and fall to the ground |
| **Cooldown** | 5 Seconds | You cannot spam the Taser; timing is critical |
| **Ammo Type** | Single Cartridge | Must reload after every successful or missed shot |
| **Wire Break** | Distance-based | Moving too far from a hit target breaks the connection |

When you fire the Taser, you must remain relatively stationary or move slowly to maintain the wire connection. If the suspect is running, lead your shot slightly to account for the travel time of the probes.

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
The Taser has a significant cooldown. If you miss your first shot during a foot pursuit, the suspect will likely gain enough distance to escape before you can fire again. Practice your aim at the station's firing range if available.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Tips for Police Officers">

To truly master the Taser, you need to understand the physics of the game. When a suspect "ragdolls," they lose all momentum and drop straight down. This is the perfect window to switch to your handcuffs and secure the arrest.

- **The Quick-Switch**: Practice switching from your Taser to Handcuffs (usually slots 2 and 3) rapidly. You only have a few seconds while the suspect is on the ground.
- **Reloading Discipline**: Never holster an empty Taser. After firing, immediately reload so you are ready for the next encounter.
- **Distance Management**: If you are too close, the suspect might punch you before you can fire. If you are too far, the probes won't reach. Maintain a "Goldilocks" distance of about 10 feet.
