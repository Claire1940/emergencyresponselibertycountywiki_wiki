The world of Liberty County is filled with hidden nods, developer inside jokes, and community references. This little amphibian has a long history of migrations across the map as the game evolved. Additionally, players can search for the elusive **Bigfoot**, a client-side anomaly that appears in randomized locations and vanishes as soon as you get close.

### The Spring Lake Frog Migration
The frog's journey is a testament to the map's physical evolution. It was first spotted in 2019 tucked away under the Freedom Avenue Bridge. As the map expanded, the developers moved it to various aquatic locations, culminating in its current resting place on the fishing dock along Riverside Drive.

### Finding Bigfoot
Bigfoot is unique because it does not spawn in a single fixed location for everyone. Instead, each player has a randomized spawn point. When you approach the creature, it immediately despawns, making it one of the most difficult secrets to photograph.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
When hunting Bigfoot, keep your camera zoomed out. Because Bigfoot vanishes upon close approach, spotting him from a distance is the only way to get a clear view before he disappears.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="River Cave and Environmental Sign Evolution">

The environmental storytelling in Liberty County often changes with major seasonal updates. The river cave, a popular spot for explorers, has featured a shifting series of warnings that reflect the developers' sense of humor and the game's changing lore. 

Originally, players who ventured deep into the river cave would find a ominous sign reading **"Never Return"**. This sign was later replaced during a major map expansion with an even more hostile message: **"WHY DID YOU COME HERE"**. In the Summer Update of 2024, the sign took on a more realistic, roleplay-friendly tone to fit the game's mining mechanics.

| Map Era | Cave Sign Text | Significance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Classic Era | Never Return | Original spooky warning |
| Map Expansion Era | WHY DID YOU COME HERE | Developer response to explorers |
| Summer Update 2024 | Keep Out! Cave mining in progress! | Roleplay-aligned warning |

### The GBUSTER License Plate Legacy
Another fascinating piece of environmental lore is the **GBUSTER** license plate. Originally introduced during the 2020 Halloween event, this plate default-spawned on the Ectomobile (a vehicle clearly based on the iconic Ghostbusters car). When the Ectomobile was temporarily replaced by the Ford Bronco, the developers transferred the license plate to the Bronco as a legacy nod.

| Vehicle | Era | Plate Text | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ectomobile | Halloween 2020 | GBUSTER | Event Exclusive |
| Ford Bronco | Post-Event | GBUSTER | Legacy Transfer |
| Ectomobile | Halloween 2021 | GBUSTER | Free Quest Reward |

<Callout type="info" title="Note on Vehicle Plates">
The GBUSTER license plate is one of the few holiday-themed cosmetic items that transitioned permanently into the game's vehicle database, serving as a badge of honor for veteran players.
</Callout>

### Minor Legacy Oddities
Other honorable mentions in the removed category include the **Tree Decal**—a round character decal left on a tree because a developer reportedly did not know how to remove it—and the custom **Tombstone Usernames** found in the local graveyard, which featured names of early community contributors.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Developer Jokes and Seasonal Spooks">

The developers of Police Roleplay Community (PRC) have always maintained a close relationship with their player base. This connection has occasionally manifested as humorous in-game assets, some of which were created as direct challenges or inside jokes for the community.

One legendary example is **RR788's Easter Egg**. RR788 was a prominent Discord member known for quickly finding every secret the developers hid. In response, developer Shawnyg created an NPC labeled "rr is a noob" and challenged them to find it. The NPC was hidden so well that it was never officially found in-game; its existence is only verified by a screenshot Shawnyg posted on Discord.

### Halloween 2024 Developer Cameo
During the 2024 Halloween Event, players who completed the final interaction could peer into a hidden hole in the map. Hidden deep beneath the textures was a model of developer **vexqty's face**, serving as a creepy, hidden jump scare for players who dared to look beyond the boundaries of the event space.
