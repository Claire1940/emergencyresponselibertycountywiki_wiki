<Section icon={Target} title="How to Master Drifting in ERLC">

Drifting is an advanced driving technique that requires a specific sequence of **Emergency Response Liberty County controls**. According to expert drivers, the Falcon Stallion 350 is one of the best vehicles for practicing these maneuvers due to its rear-wheel-drive characteristics and high torque.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Disable Safety Systems">
    Before attempting a drift, you must disable the vehicle's electronic assistants. Press **Y** to turn off ABS and **T** to turn off Traction Control (TCS). This allows the wheels to spin freely and slide.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Approach and Speed">
    Accelerate to a speed between **40 and 70 miles per hour**. Approaching a corner too fast will result in a spin-out, while going too slow will not provide enough momentum to break traction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate the Turn">
    As you reach the apex of the corner, turn sharply using **A** or **D** while simultaneously holding the **Left Shift** key (Handbrake) for a brief second to kick the tail out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Counter-Steer and Throttle">
    Once the car begins to slide, steer in the opposite direction of the turn to balance the car. Feather the **W** key to maintain the slide without spinning 360 degrees.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Drift Mastery">
Successful drifting is about the balance between the handbrake (Shift) and the throttle (W). Practice in the industrial district where there is less traffic and wider turns.
</Callout>

</Section>
