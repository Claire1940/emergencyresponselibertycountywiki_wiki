<Section icon={Settings} title="Step-by-Step Server Configuration">

Setting up your first instance among the many **Emergency Response Liberty County roleplay servers** involves several technical steps. You must first purchase a private server through the Roblox game page and then configure the internal settings via the ERLC menu.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Purchase and Naming">
    Navigate to the ERLC game page on Roblox and purchase a Private Server. Once active, enter the game and open the Server Settings menu. Give your server a unique name that reflects your community (e.g., "Liberty County Realistic RP").
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Department Configuration">
    Decide which departments will be active. You can toggle access for Police, Sheriff, Fire/Rescue, and DOT. In 2026, it is highly recommended to keep DOT active as they now handle all infrastructure repairs and towing, which adds significant realism to traffic accident scenes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Permission and Staff Setup">
    Assign roles to your trusted community members. Use the "Staff Permissions" tab to grant specific access levels. Be cautious with the "Server Owner" level permissions; only grant these to individuals you trust with the server's longevity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Applying Custom Packs">
    If your community has the budget, equip the Custom Livery Pack and the API Pack. These allow you to upload unique department decals and connect your server to Discord bots for real-time logs and player tracking.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success Strategy">
Always perform a "Soft Launch" with 5-10 players to test your staff permissions and custom liveries before announcing a major community event.

<Section icon={Users} title="Staff Hierarchy and Management">

A server is only as good as its staff. For **Emergency Response Liberty County roleplay servers**, a clear hierarchy prevents administrative abuse and ensures that roleplay scenes are handled professionally. Most top-tier servers utilize a three-tier staff system to manage the 50-player server capacity effectively.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Moderators">
    - **Primary Task**: Scene management and rule enforcement.
    - **Tools**: Basic kick commands, vehicle despawning.
    - **Role**: First responders to "FailRP" or "RDM" complaints.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Administrators">
    - **Primary Task**: Dispute resolution and staff oversight.
    - **Tools**: Ban commands, server-wide announcements.
    - **Role**: Managing the Moderator team and approving liveries.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Department Leads">
    - **Primary Task**: Training and SOP development.
    - **Tools**: Rank management within their specific team.
    - **Role**: Ensuring the Police or Fire teams act realistically.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

| Command Category | Common Commands | Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Environment** | /time [hr], /weather [type] | Setting the scene for night patrols or storm rescues. |
